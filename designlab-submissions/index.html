DesignLab Submissions

Here are some of my submissions to each project in DesignLab. All of these are made with Sketch, even though I'm fairly new to it.

Project # 1

Project brief: design a landing page.
You're a designer who just got your first creative brief from a client.

They're a premium food brand that launched a new smoothie product called SuperGood. The client wants to: 1) raise awareness of their brand and product benefits, and 2) collect email addresses for their mailing list so they can keep people informed of product updates.

Your task is to design a page that meets these requirements. Some basic copy, provided by the client, is displayed on the canvas on the right. Feel free to get creative with colors, font choices, layout, additional content and images, and the size of elements -- do what you need to make the client happy and fulfill the project requirements!

Project # 2

Visual hierarchy.
Take a look at the set of content on the right — a timeline for a microblogging application with different types of information (post content, author, post time, sharing links). Arrange the information in a manner you feel appropriately utilizes the concept of visual hierarchy.
Don’t worry about font selection or color palettes, just on the importance of the content and conveying meaning through the hierarchy techniques you read about in this unit. Play around with weight, spacing, alignment, contrast (shades of grey), and try to simplify your design as much as possible while still conveying hierarchy.

Project # 3

A blog post with different types of information (post content, author, post date). Arrange the information in a manner you feel appropriately utilizes the concept of visual hierarchy.
Play around with weight, spacing, alignment, contrast (shades of grey), and try to simplify your design as much as possible while still conveying hierarchy.

Project # 4

Visual hierarchy, take 3!
A homepage for a news listings site (think Digg or Reddit). Arrange the information in a manner you feel appropriately utilizes the concept of visual hierarchy.
